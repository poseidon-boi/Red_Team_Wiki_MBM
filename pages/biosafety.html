Overview

We are dealing with genetically modified bacteria, therefore biocontainment as well as safety is equally essential. For the biocontainment of our genetically modified bacterium, a kill switch can be developed which makes sure that these genetically modified bacteria proliferate uncontrolled in the environment. We plan on using an inducible kill switch system that involves an inducible gene expression system using Xylose as an inducer. In the absence of xylose it causes toxicity to our chassis which ultimately helps in the biocontainment of our bacteria . MurE gene that encodes UDP-N-acetylmuramoyl-L-alanyl-D-glutamyl-meso-2,6-diaminopimelate synthetase which is an essential precursor for peptidoglycan synthesis. In its absence bacteria are unable to synthesise cell wall leading to its death [1]. We can replace the natural promote of this MurE gene ( NCBI Gene ID: 937567) with PxylA (Xylose-Inducible Promoter).The XylR-PxylA system is adapted for use in Bacillus subtilis , provides a mechanism for tightly controlled, xylose-inducible gene expression [2].

Mechanism: XylR (Xylose Repressor) is Encoded by the xylR gene, XylR is a repressor protein that binds to the PxylA promoter in the absence of xylose This binding physically blocks RNA polymerase from initiating transcription, thus repressing gene expression.

PxylA (Xylose-Inducible Promoter):This promoter drives the expression of genes that are kept downstream. Which will essentially be our MurE gene.

When xylose is present, xylose binds to XylR, causing a conformational change in the repressor. This change prevents XylR from binding to the PxylA promoter, thus relieving the repression and allowing RNA polymerase to bind and initiate transcription of the downstream gene. The level of gene expression can be modulated by varying the concentration of xylose [3].

We propose to add xylose(white wettable powder) [4] with our bacteria. Implementation methodologies are still to be determined.
